## 6. Profile Differentiation — Core Research Pillars

**1. Trustworthy AI & Computational Fairness**  
Primary research identity — auditing T2I models for demographic drift, disability bias, and CFR-V dissociation. Distinct from generic ML engineering. Directly aligns with MPI-INF, DFKI, and German public university ethics mandates.

**2. Applied Modeler Paradigm**  
Academic footprint optimized around macro-level analytical modeling, data representation, and algorithmic fairness — not enterprise DevOps or low-level system engineering.

**3. Physical World Motivation**  
Preference for computational architectures solving tangible problems: biological structure analysis, satellite remote sensing, robotic navigation, and bias mitigation in generative vision systems.

**4. Ethical AI & Data Privacy**  
Strong commitment to open-source tooling, localized LLM systems, and algorithmic transparency — directly resonant with Germany's strict data privacy standards and GDPR research culture.
