Text-to-image (T2I) models aligned via RLHF and Prompt Tuning often generate demographically diverse faces, distribute representations across groups, and avoid common stereotypes. But standard attribute distribution metrics primarily evaluate surface traits and often fail to capture the deeper sociotechnical failures, such as contextual erasure of intersectional identities. In order to bridge this gap, we introduce a paired counterfactual audit framework that compares neutral profession images against disability-conditioned counterparts by fixing the latent seeds.
